Do you recall the Bill Clinton album cover trend that took over Instagram for a couple weeks?

While short lived, this Instagram challenge trend is indicative of the way that music has embedded itself in our modern day culture. Discussions about favorite artists and banger tracks can be found in everyday casual discussions everywhere.

People fill their social media with various screenshots from Spotify and Apple Music to show off the music that they currently listen to. Whether we like it or not, the music we listen to is tied to our identity as people, which we have learned to use to build relationships with others and discuss the music that we‚Äôre passionate about.

There are a wide varierty of social platforms...

...that utilize different mediums of relationship building. However, there isn't a dedicated platform that builds on this idea of letting the music you listen to curate your online profile.

Based on this conclusion, a couple friends and I set out to create a social platform where you can connect with others and explore new music based on your favorite music.

Going Into This Project...

...I had no formal experience in graphic design. I was initially tasked with frontend dev, but I was really eager to open myself up to the world of design. Thanks to the help of some fellow team members who did have some basic design experience, we curated a roadmap that we could follow to get to a final design.

The group had a POC version of the application already, but had made plans to scrap the old design and rebuild the application and brand design from the ground up, preserving the features and functionality that had been working from the previous version of the app.

In Retrospect...

...there are a ton of things I would do differently, which is undestandable given that this was my first semi-formal design project. To begin with I would have put more focus on user research and user testing. Far too many of our team's decisions were based on our own opinions that led us to dead-ends and impossible tasks. We failed in many areas to introduce contraints with both our designs and applciation features, which was the main contributor to us not being able to finish and publish our application.
